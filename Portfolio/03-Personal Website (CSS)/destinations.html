Tokyo, Japan

  • Region: Kanto
  • Country: Japan
  • Population: 13.96 Millions
  • Latitude/Longitude: latitude 35.6895 y longitude 139.69171

Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital, mixes the ultra-modern and the traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The opulent Shinto Meiji Shrine is known for its towering gate and surrounding forests. The Imperial Palace is located in the middle of large public gardens. The city's various museums offer exhibits ranging from classical art (at the Tokyo National Museum) to a reconstructed kabuki theater (at the Edo-Tokyo Museum).

Bruges, Belgium

  • Province: Occidental Flandes
  • Country: Belgium
  • Population: 120 thousands
  • Latitude/Longitude: latitude 51.2089200 y longitude 3.2242400

Bruges, the capital of West Flanders in northwest Belgium, is distinguished by its canals, cobblestone streets and medieval buildings. Its port, Zeebrugge, is an important European fishing and trade center. On the city center's Burg Square, the 14th-century Stadhuis (town hall) has an ornately carved ceiling. Nearby, Markt Square has a 13th-century bell tower with a 47-bell organ and an 83m-high tower with panoramic views.

Shanghai, China

  • Province: Any - Shanghai's Municipality
  • Country: China
  • Population: 26.32 millions
  • Latitude/Longitude: latitude 31.22222 y longitude 121.45806

Shanghai, on China's central coast, is the country's largest city and a global financial hub. Its center is the Bund, a famous coastal promenade with buildings from the colonial era. Across the Huangpu River rises the futuristic skyline of the Pudong district, with the 632m-high Shanghai Tower and the Oriental Pearl TV Tower, with its distinctive pink spheres. The sprawling Yu Garden has traditional pavilions, towers and ponds.

Singapore, Singapore

  • Republic of Singapore
  • Country: Singapur
  • Population: 5.637 millions
  • Latitude/Longitude: latitude 1.352083 y longitude 103.819836

Singapore, officially known as the Republic of Singapore, is a unique blend of city-state and island country, located in the maritime heart of Southeast Asia. This strategic geographical position, nestled between the Malacca Strait, the Singapore Strait, the South China Sea and the Johor Strait, has shaped its history and continues to influence its current trajectory. The country's territory includes a main island and 63 satellite islands and islets and one peripheral one.
